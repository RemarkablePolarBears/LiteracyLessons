Structure of a Research Paper

  • One paragraph per unit:
    • Thesis statement
    • Topic sentence 1 + detail (Study 1)
    • Topic sentence 2 + detail (Study 2)
    • Topic sentence 3 + detail (Study 3)
    • Concluding statement

Abstract Writing

  • Include niche, purpose, data, results
  • Use keywords (e.g., 'scarce' = niche)
  • Align DV & IV with purpose & results

Discussion & Conclusion

  • Summarize findings & broader inferences
  • Compare with prior studies
  • Contributions & implications (pedagogy, policy, theory)
  • Limitations & future research
  • Use present tense for facts & implications

Method

  • Participants: background, recruitment, ethics
  • Design: procedures
  • Tools: technology used
  • Data collection: variables, instruments, bias control
  • Analysis: descriptive stats, missing data, software

Enhancements

  • Break DV & IV into levels/functions
  • Indicators & clusters examples

Verb Tenses for Each Section

  • Abstract: Past & Present
  • Introduction: Present & Present Perfect
  • Literature Review: Past & Present
  • Method: Past (actions) & Present (figures)
  • Results: Past (findings) & Present (interpretation)
  • Discussion/Conclusion: Past (summary) & Present (implications)

Results

  • Start with overview statement
  • Provide examples (tables/figures)
  • Elaborate key points & answer RQs
  • Titles aligned to benefits/processes/difficulties
  • Write past tense & be concise/objective
